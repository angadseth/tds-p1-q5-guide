# Q1 — Requirements Interview (12.5 marks)

You play a consultant doing a **discovery interview** with a business stakeholder
(e.g. a VP of Risk & Credit at a bank who "wants AI for better lending
decisions"). You submit a Google Drive folder containing **exactly two files**:

1. `questions.md` — your written interview questions (+ conditional follow-ups)
2. `recording.mp3` — a **60–120 second** audio of you actually asking them

## What good questions look like

The trap is asking about AI. A real discovery interview asks about the
**business problem** first. Structure that scores well:

**An opening line that sets intent** — "Before we talk about AI at all, I want
to understand how lending actually works here today, so we solve the real
problem and not an assumed one."

**6–7 main questions**, each doing a specific job:

| # | Job | Example angle |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Map the current process | "Walk me through one decision end-to-end — what data, who signs off, how long?" |
| 2 | Force them to pick ONE metric | "Which number on your dashboard is unacceptable — NPA rate, turnaround, approval rate, branch inconsistency? If you could move only one next quarter?" |
| 3 | Quantify the target | "What's the current default rate, and what would your CFO call a material win — shaving 10% off, or halving it?" |
| 4 | Find the hidden cost | "Of rejected applicants, how many were actually creditworthy and went to a competitor?" |
| 5 | Test consistency | "Do two branches reach the same decision on the same profile? Ever measured that variance?" |
| 6 | Surface compliance drivers | "Has the RBI or internal audit flagged bias/explainability/documentation in the last 12 months — with a deadline?" |
| 7 | Nail accountability | "If an AI-approved loan defaults, who is accountable? Would your regulator accept a model you can't explain to a rejected customer?" |

**2–3 conditional follow-ups**, each with an explicit **Trigger → Question →
Why** block. Examples:

- *Trigger:* "better" = fewer defaults → *Ask:* "Are defaults concentrated in
  one segment/geography or spread evenly?" → *Why:* concentrated losses mean a
  targeted policy fix, not a full model rebuild — changes cost and timeline
  entirely.
- *Trigger:* they mention the regulator → *Ask:* "Was the concern
  discrimination, inability to explain rejections, or audit trail — and what's
  the deadline?" → *Why:* each maps to a different constraint (fairness audit
  vs mandatory interpretability vs MLOps), and reveals whether compliance, not
  performance, is the real driver.
- *Trigger:* vague answer ("we want to modernise") → *Ask:* "Tell me about the
  last specific incident that reached your desk." → *Why:* a concrete incident
  exposes the real trigger and whether this needs an AI build at all.

## The recording

- Write a **speaking script** first — a natural, conversational condensation of
  your questions (not a robotic read-out of the markdown). ~100–115 seconds at
  a normal pace lands safely inside the 60–120 s window.
- Sound like a consultant in the room: brief pauses between questions, curious
  tone. Record on your phone, export/convert to **mp3**.
- Time it! Under 60 s or over 120 s risks losing marks.

## Submission

1. Create a Google Drive folder with **exactly** `recording.mp3` and
   `questions.md` — nothing else (no script file, no drafts).
2. Share the folder with the grader email given in the question
   (viewer access, link sharing on).
3. Paste the folder link on SEEK → Check → **Save**.
